## Description

When designing a product, it's important to start by defining clear and specific objectives. These objectives should be based on the needs and wants of the target audience for the product, as well as the business goals of the organization creating the product.

To define objectives with a product design approach, you can follow these steps:

1. **Identify the target audience for the product.** Who will be using it, and what are their needs and preferences?
2. **Determine the business goals of the organization creating the product.** What are they trying to achieve with the product, and how will it help them meet their business objectives?
3. **Develop a set of specific and measurable objectives for the product.** Based on the target audience and business goals, develop **SMART** objectives: Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, and Time-bound.
4. **Use these objectives to guide the design process.** As you develop different design concepts and prototypes, evaluate them against the objectives to ensure that they are meeting the needs of the target audience and aligning with the business goals.
5. **Continuously refine and update the objectives** as needed, based on feedback from user testing, market research, and other sources.

Overall, defining clear and specific objectives with a product design approach can help ensure that the final product is effective, user-friendly, and aligned with the goals of the organization creating it.
