### Description

---

The Google HEART framework is a set of design principles that focus on the user experience. It was developed by Google, and comprises five core principles: focus on the user, simplify, be useful, be beautiful, and work like magic.

- **Adoption**: Adoption is measured by the number of new users over time, or the percentage of customers using new features. For example: number of people who completed the onboarding process and became regular users.
- **Engagement**: How often do people return to use your product? It can be measured by visits per user per week, session duration, or key actions (e.g. visits, number of photos uploaded).
- **Happiness**: How do users perceive your product? Usually measured by surveys, ratings and reviews, NPS.
- **Retention**: What percentage of users return to your product? Customer retention is measured by churn.
- **Task Success**: Can users accomplish their goals and tasks quickly and easily? Task success depends on factors such as efficiency (the time it takes users to complete a task), effectiveness (percentage of tasks completed), and error rate.
