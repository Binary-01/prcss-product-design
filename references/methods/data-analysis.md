# 🔍 Data Analysis

**Phase:** Discover · **Emoji:** 🔍

> Verbatim Design Method from the PRCSS* template. The emoji reflects the primary phase this method belongs to.

### Description

---

During the discovery phase of a product, data analysis can be used to gain a better understanding of the problem the product is trying to solve, the target market for the product, and potential solutions to the problem. This can be done by collecting and analyzing data from a variety of sources, such as market research, customer surveys, and industry reports.

To begin, identify the problem that the product is trying to solve and define the target market for the product. This will help to focus the data analysis and ensure that the data collected is relevant to the product.

Next, collect data from a variety of sources that can provide insight into the problem and target market. This can include market research reports, customer surveys, and data from existing products in the same industry.

Once the data has been collected, it can be analyzed using a variety of tools and techniques, such as statistical analysis, data visualization, and machine learning algorithms. These tools can help to identify trends, patterns, and insights in the data that can inform the development of the product.

Throughout the process, it is important to involve stakeholders, such as product managers, designers, and engineers, to ensure that the data analysis aligns with the goals of the product and the needs of the target market. By using data analysis during the discovery phase, product teams can make informed decisions about the direction of the product and increase the chances of success.
