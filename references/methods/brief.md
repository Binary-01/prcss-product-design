## Description

In a product approach, a design brief is a document that outlines the requirements, objectives, and constraints for a product design project. It serves as a guide for the design team and helps them understand the client's needs and expectations for the product. A product design brief typically includes the following steps:

1. **Identify the product's goals and objectives.** The first step in creating a design brief is to clearly define the product's goals and objectives. This will help the design team understand what the client wants to achieve with the product and ensure that the final design meets their expectations.
2. **Define the target market.** The design brief should also include information about the target market for the product. This will help the design team create a design that is relevant and appealing to the intended audience.
3. **Outline the project's constraints.** The design brief should also include any constraints or limitations that the design team needs to be aware of. For example, the brief may specify a limited budget, a specific deadline, or other factors that could impact the design process.
4. **Provide background information.** The design brief should also provide background information about the product, such as the company or organization it is for, the context in which the product will be used, and any relevant history or previous design work.
5. **Request specific design elements.** The design brief should also include any specific design elements that the client wants to see in the final product. For example, the brief may request a specific color palette, materials, or style.
6. **Include any relevant reference materials.** Finally, the design brief should include any relevant reference materials, such as examples of similar products, sketches or mock-ups, or other materials that the design team can use as inspiration. This will help the team understand the client's vision for the product and create a design that aligns with their goals.
